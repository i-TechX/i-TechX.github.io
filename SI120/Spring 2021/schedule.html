Description è¯¾ç¨‹ç®€ä»‹

This course will focus on the basic notions, theories, methods and applications of discrete structures. It will cover several important topics in discrete mathematics including mathematical logic, number theory, Combinatorics, and graph theory. This course will provide solid mathematical foundations for many future courses. Logic plays an important role in software engineering, programming, artificial intelligence and database theory. Proofs by induction and more general mathematical proofs are ubiquitous in theory of computation, compiler design and formal grammars. The techniques of combinatorial counting are used frequently in computer science to obtain formulas and estimates in the analysis of algorithms. Number theory and related algorithms are central to modern cryptography. Discrete structures such as sets, graphs and trees are fundamental tools in software engineering.
